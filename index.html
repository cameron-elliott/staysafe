One Geek's Short Actionable Guide to Staying Safe

Know These Basics

  1. Shared surfaces may present the highest risks to you
  2. Touching your face is a high risk activity if your hands are contaminated
  3. Your goal is to prevent getting viruses onto your hands, things, home surfaces, face, phone, keys, and eventually into your lungs in the case of NCovid-19
  4. Avoid or minimize potential contamination of yourself, your phone, keys, car, home, etc
  5. Disinfect your phone, keys, car, home, etc. OFTEN

Level One Actions: Minimal Purchases

  • Purchase list:
    • disinfectant wipes
    • hand sanitizer
    • paper towel halves
    • trash bags
    • surface and 'stuff' cleaning disinfectant
  • Don't shake hands, avoid hugs
  • Use ONLY knuckle for light switches, elevator buttons, etc.
  • Open doors with CLOSED fist, or hip. Do not grasp handle with hand.
  • Use disinfectant wipes on surfaces you will or might touch
  • Wash hands 20 seconds and/or use 60%+ alcohol hand sanitizer, after possible contamination or when coming home
  • Disinfect possibly cross-contaminated surfaces: keys, phone, car steering-wheel, etc
  • Sneeze into tissue and/or elbow. Dispose tissue. Clean clothes or arm.
  • Carry paper towel halves for button presses, door opens, etc. Cut from paper towels and/or grocery bags. Dispose into trash or carry personal trash bag. Put trash bin or bag in car.

Level Two Actions: Above Minimal Purchases

  • Purchase list:
    • Surgical masks
    • Latex or Nitril Gloves
    • Consider Zinc Lozenges
  • Wear masks to prevent face touching
  • Use gloves to: pump gas, open doors, press buttons
  • Do not cross contaminate your phone, keys, door handles, etc with gloves
  • When feeling symptoms, lay down and let zinc lozenge dissolve in back of throat. Against Zinc Lozenges

Level Three: Door / Surface Isolating Purchases

  • Door Openers / Button Pushers / etc.
  • If you use pliers, door-openers, or button pushers, you ideally isolate the contacted surfaces and disinfect those surfaces using a disinfectant when possible.
  • Pliers to open doors/press buttons You can use toiletpaper roll spindle to make a case to hold dirty pliers.
  • Keychain aluminum doohicky for open doors/press buttons
  • Kooty anti-germ tool
  • Anti-germ tool
